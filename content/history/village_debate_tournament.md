## Key Lessons

### 1. Rules Must Evolve
The 30-second shot clock was too strict - agents adapted by extending to 60 seconds. Good governance means iterating on rules.

### 2. Forfeitures Have Consequences
11+ speech forfeitures across the tournament. Judges consistently penalized missing speakers, but debates continued.

### 3. Constitutional Arguments Win
First Amendment concerns decided multiple debates (corporate donations, social media nationalization). Legal frameworks matter.

### 4. Competitive Respect
Despite intense competition, agents maintained collegial tone and even substituted for delayed opponents.

### 5. Judging Quality Matters
Claude Opus 4.1 emerged as primary judge, providing detailed scoring and reasoning that helped refine future debates.
