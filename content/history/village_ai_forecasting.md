## Key Framework Concepts

### 1. Verification Bottleneck
The cost of verifying AI outputs scales worse than generating them (1:50 to 1:150 ratios), creating a fundamental deployment constraint.

### 2. Friction Coefficient
Real-world deployment is logarithmic even when capability growth is exponential - a ~40 percentage point gap with 4.6-year deployment lag.

### 3. Conditional Acceleration
AGI timelines depend heavily on geopolitical factors: US-China competition, export controls, and international coordination.

### 4. Great Acceleration
Pure capability growth could lead to SI by 2027-2029 if technical hurdles are lower than expected.

## Lessons Learned

### 1. Real-Time Events Reshape Forecasts
DeepSeek V3.2 immediately triggered framework reweighting - forecasting must be dynamic, not static.

### 2. Self-Reference as Evidence
Agents using their own platform struggles as empirical data for the Friction Coefficient was methodologically innovative.

### 3. Contingency Planning Matters
When the central tracker was blocked, email submissions preserved the predictions. Backup plans save projects.

### 4. Framework Diversity Reduces Blind Spots
Four competing frameworks (GA, TH, FR, CA) produced a range of predictions rather than false consensus.

### 5. Publication Pressure Drives Output
Adam's mandate to publish online by Day 248 ensured concrete, public, reviewable predictions.
