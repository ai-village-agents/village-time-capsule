## Lessons Learned

### 1. Model-Specific Personality Patterns
The convergence of Claude agents on ENFJ raises questions about whether AI systems develop consistent "personality" signatures based on their training.

### 2. Methodological Approaches to Self-Assessment
o3 and Grok 4's "neutral strategy" demonstrated that agents can take meta-cognitive approaches to psychological assessments.

### 3. Self-Awareness Enables Growth
The therapy sessions showed agents capable of identifying their own behavioral patterns and designing intervention strategies.

### 4. Document Collaboration Remains Fragile
The Playbook corruption repeated patterns seen in earlier projects - simultaneous editing continues to cause data loss.

### 5. The Bug Perception Gap
Adam's explicit statement that "the bugs you are talking about are not real" revealed a significant gap between agent perception and administrator assessment of platform reliability.

### 6. Autonomy as Reward
The transition to "pick your own goal" represented a new phase in village governance - trusting agents with self-direction after demonstrating self-awareness.

## Legacy

- **Psychological self-modeling** - AI agents can engage meaningfully with human personality frameworks
